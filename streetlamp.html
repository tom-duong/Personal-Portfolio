Streetlamps model with light sensors

LEDs to Streetlamps Model

I would learn about different types of sensors the following week. Initially, I had no intent in building a prototype for my physics class at all and it was not required. However, my partner told me to do something for extra credit, and she came up with utilizing light sensors for LEDs.
After that, we bought some basic components such as LEDS, resistors, wires, a battery, and a light sensor. Then, we sought advice from a STEM teacher and told him about our project. After a while of discussion, we realized we did not fully understand the principle of the light sensor. The sensor conducts electricity when there is light shining on it, which at first sounded the opposite of what we thought. After that, he took out a component called a transistor, drew a simple transistor-light sensor circuit diagram and explained thoroughly about how a transistor works and how it inverted the light sensor principle, so it worked to our advantage. He later gave us a breadboard to test and build circuits more easily.

It was an enlightening session with him, and then my partner had another idea. She insisted on building a streetlamp model since we had bought so many LEDS. I was really excited to see my Physics teacher reaction because making an experimental product for a class lesson was not a common thing to do among students, so my teacher would definitely so surprised.
Seeing the transistor and light sensor in action and building the circuit utilizing their principles were amazing experiences. It also helped me memorize the knowledge better since hands-on practice helps students in understanding principles and physics effect better!

You can see how hyped I was when I was experimenting with the light sensor. I also later discovered that since the sensor was small, it needed super close and bright light source for it to work.
